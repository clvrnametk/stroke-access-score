About This Project

Mapping the Geography of Stroke Care

The Stroke Access Score is a research tool built to quantify geographic inequity in access to stroke-certified hospitals across California — and to examine how that inequity intersects with race, income, and clinical risk.

The Hypothesis

Time-to-treatment is the single most critical determinant of stroke outcome. A patient receiving clot-busting therapy within 60 minutes of stroke onset ("golden hour") is dramatically more likely to survive without disability than one who arrives later. The location of certified stroke centers is therefore not an abstract planning concern — it is a life-and-death variable.

Urban proximity bias in U.S. hospital siting means that rural, low-income, and minority communities are systematically farther from certified stroke centers — and that this geographic inequity compounds the clinical stroke risk factors already concentrated in those same communities.

This project tests that hypothesis empirically for every ZIP code in California using drive-time data, Census demographics, and CMS hospital quality ratings as a proxy for stroke certification capability.

How the Score Works

Each hospital is assigned a stroke-care tier based on its CMS overall quality rating (a proxy for certification, pending EMNet data). The four tiers follow the national stroke center hierarchy:

Level Description Base Score Penalty >30 min Penalty >60 min
CSC Comprehensive Stroke Center — full thrombectomy + complex care 100 −30 −15
TSC Thrombectomy-capable Stroke Center 75 −15 −15
PSC Primary Stroke Center — IV tPA capable 50 −15 −15
ASRH Acute Stroke Ready Hospital — stabilization + transfer 25 −20 −15

For each ZIP code, the SAS score is the highest adjusted score across all hospitals in the drive-time cache — i.e., the score reflects the best facility realistically reachable from that ZIP. Penalties are cumulative: a ZIP that is 70 minutes from its nearest CSC receives a score of 100 − 30 − 15 = 55.

Vulnerability Flags

Three flags identify ZIP codes where poor access intersects with community-level risk factors.

SEV — Socioeconomic Vulnerability

SAS score < 40, majority-minority community, AND poverty rate > 20% or uninsured rate > 15%.

CRF — Clinical Risk Factor

SAS score < 40, AND median age > 40 — indicating elevated population-level stroke risk.

Double Jeopardy

Both SEV and CRF flags are active — communities facing simultaneously poor access and high stroke risk burden.

Data Sources

  • CMS Hospital Compare
    Hospital characteristics, overall quality ratings (1–5), and facility type for all 378 California hospitals. Used to infer stroke certification tier as a proxy (EMNet certification data pending).
  • U.S. Census — ACS 5-Year Estimates, 2022
    ZIP Code Tabulation Area (ZCTA) level demographics including total population, race/ethnicity breakdowns, median household income, poverty rate, health insurance coverage, and median age.
  • Census TIGER/Line Shapefiles, 2022
    ZCTA boundary geometries used for choropleth mapping and geographic area calculations. Geometries simplified to 0.005° tolerance for web performance.
  • Census 2020 Decennial — Block-level Population
    PL 94-171 redistricting data used to compute population-weighted centroids for each ZCTA, replacing geometric centroids for more accurate geographic representation of where people actually live.
  • Drive-Time Cache
    8,986 precomputed origin-destination drive times between California ZCTAs and the 338 geocoded hospitals. Distances computed via point-to-point routing. Collection methodology pending documentation.
  • Census Geocoder API
    Free batch geocoding used to assign latitude/longitude to the 378 hospital addresses. 338 of 378 (89.4%) were successfully geocoded.

Limitations & Caveats

  • CMS ratings as proxy: Hospital overall ratings are used as a substitute for formal stroke center certification (CSC/TSC/PSC/ASRH from The Joint Commission or DNV). These are correlated but not identical. Actual certification data from EMNet will improve accuracy in future versions.
  • Drive-time estimates: Times are point-to-centroid estimates under typical traffic conditions — not EMS routing times, which vary by dispatch location, traffic, and availability. Actual ambulance travel times will differ.
  • ZCTA-level resolution: ZIP Code Tabulation Areas are administrative units averaging several square miles. Within a single ZCTA, access can vary substantially. The score represents an average condition, not any individual's experience.
  • Static snapshot: Scores are based on 2022 ACS data and a single drive-time collection. Hospital closures, new certifications, or demographic shifts are not reflected.
  • No weighting for hospital capacity: A ZIP 10 minutes from a CSC with 4 stroke beds is scored the same as one 10 minutes from a CSC with 40 beds. Capacity constraints are not modeled.
  • Not for clinical or emergency use: This tool is for research and policy analysis only. It should not be used to guide individual patient routing or emergency medical decisions.
