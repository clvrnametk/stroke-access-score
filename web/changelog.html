Data Update #1

LA County EMS Certification Correction

Published: June 8, 2026  ·  Source: Los Angeles County Emergency Medical Services Approved Stroke Center Directory, July 28, 2025

Replaced CMS quality rating proxy with official LA County EMS stroke certification data for 51 LA County hospitals. This is the first step toward full EMNet certification integration statewide.

51 Hospitals matched & updated
448 ZIP codes re-scored
218 Scores increased
34 Scores decreased

What changed and why

The original Stroke Access Score pipeline used CMS Hospital Compare overall quality ratings (1–5 stars) as a proxy for stroke center certification — assigning CSC to 5-star hospitals, TSC to 4-star hospitals, and PSC to all others. This was an explicit approximation, documented in the methodology, pending access to authoritative certification data.

This update replaces that proxy for LA County hospitals with the LA County EMS Approved Stroke Center Directory — the official list of facilities verified by the county's Emergency Medical Services agency. For the 51 hospitals matched, the tier assignment now reflects actual certification level rather than a quality rating estimate.

EMNet national stroke certification data remains pending and will trigger a full statewide update when it arrives.

Data Update #2

Hospital Dataset Cleanup

Published: June 8, 2026

Two additional corrections were made to the LA County hospital dataset following clinical review. The TSC certification tier was removed statewide as a proxy artifact, and non-certified hospitals in LA County were removed from the dataset entirely. These corrections make the data more accurate but result in lower scores for some ZIP codes — reflecting true access, not degraded care.

63Hospitals de-certified
635ZIP codes re-scored
5Hospitals removed from LA County
0Scores increased

Correction 1 — TSC category removed statewide

The Thrombectomy-Capable Stroke Center (TSC) tier was assigned entirely by the CMS hospital quality rating proxy — not by any authoritative stroke certification source. LA County EMS and standard California EMS practice recognize only two tiers: CSC and PSC. TSC does not exist as a recognized EMS destination category in California.

Result

63 hospitals had their certification nulled statewide. 635 ZIP codes were re-scored. All score changes were flat or negative — no ZIP scored higher from this correction. 6 ZIP codes in rural Northern California were zeroed out, meaning their nearest previously-labeled facility was TSC only and no certified stroke center is within the cached drive-time radius.

Correction 2 — Non-certified LA County hospitals removed

Any hospital in LA County not appearing on the LA County EMS Approved Stroke Center Directory (July 28, 2025) was removed from the dataset entirely. A hospital not on the EMS directory is not a recognized stroke destination — it should not appear in routing or scoring.

5 hospitals removed

  • Alhambra Hospital Medical Center
  • Glendale Memorial Hospital & Health Center
  • Martin Luther King Jr. Community Hospital
  • San Gabriel Valley Medical Center
  • VA Long Beach Healthcare System

What changed and why

The original pipeline used CMS hospital data as the source of truth for which hospitals existed and what level of care they provided. CMS data is comprehensive for billing purposes but does not reflect EMS stroke certification. This update replaces LA County hospital data with the authoritative LA County EMS directory. For the rest of California, CMS data with the TSC proxy removed remains in use pending EMNet national certification data.

Score decreases in this update reflect more accurate data, not worse care. A ZIP code that previously showed a score of 75 because a TSC-labeled hospital was nearby may now show a lower score because that hospital was not actually a certified stroke destination. The lower score is correct.
Coming Next

Update #3 — EMNet National Certification Data

Update #3 will integrate EMNet national stroke certification data statewide, replacing all remaining CMS proxy ratings for hospitals outside LA County. This will be the most significant data update and will trigger a full re-score of all 1,802 California ZIP codes.
