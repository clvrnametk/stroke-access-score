Critical Findings

    

      

        Income & Access Gap
        —
        
Average SAS score difference between the top and bottom income quartile. Higher income consistently predicts better stroke care proximity.

      

      

        Racial Disparity
        —
        
Average SAS score difference between non-majority-minority and majority-minority ZIP codes. Positive = non-MM scores higher; negative = MM scores higher (urban density effect).

      

      

        Population at Risk
        —
        
Californians living in double jeopardy ZIPs — facing both poor stroke care access and elevated stroke risk factors simultaneously.
