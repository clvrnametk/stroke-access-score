Where You Live Determines Your Care

    

      The Stroke Access Score measures every California ZIP code on its proximity
      to certified stroke care. The results reveal a stark geography of inequality.

Critical Findings

    

      

        Income & Access Gap
        —
        
Average SAS score difference between top and bottom income quartile. ZIP codes in the highest income quartile average 94.1 vs 90.0 in the lowest.

      

      

        Racial Disparity
        —
        
Average SAS score difference between non-majority-minority and majority-minority ZIP codes. Non-majority-minority ZIPs average 93.6 vs majority-minority at 92.9. Note: the small gap reflects LA County's dense CSC coverage — disparity findings will be stronger in the full statewide analysis.

      

      

        Population at Risk
        —
        
Residents living in ZIP codes with limited stroke care access (score 0–39).

      

    

  







  

    Detailed Findings
    
A geography of inequality
